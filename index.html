★ THE MAY 1ST TRADITION ★
    May 1st in France:
    
    Person A hands lily → Person B
         🌸 → 😊
    = Good luck all year!

In France, May 1st is "La Fête du Muguet"—The Lily of the Valley Festival. Since 1561, people exchange these delicate flowers for good luck.

On this day, anyone can sell flowers on the streets without a license. Vendors set up everywhere, and the tradition is simple: give someone a sprig of muguet, and they'll have happiness for the whole year.

When I think of traditions—small rituals that mark time, that say "this matters"—I think of us. The way I always tell you when I get home. The way you ask what I ate that day. Our late-night calls that stretch until one of us can barely keep our eyes open.

I think of your willingness to follow these traditions/rituals. Posting me a Christmas card when you haven't even written one in years, asking me to be your Valentine, trying to order lilies of the valley for me despite their rarity in Hong Kong... And even if we are unable to exchange the flowers this year, I hope one day we'll be able to gift them to one another.

If I could start a tradition, it would be this: every May 1st, wherever we are in the world, I want to find you lily of the valley. A promise of good luck, yes. But also a reminder that you make every day feel like the return of happiness.

★ SWEET SCENT, IMPOSSIBLE TO CAPTURE ★

    Natural extraction → nearly impossible

    Dior | Chanel | Guerlain
    
    My personal favourites:
    Casablanca Lily by Byredo
    Lilyphéa by Diptyque
